About The Project:

Research was conducted for an online HR company. Mission was to find key factors that influence users in the job market (i.e job seekers). Because this project is covered by a non disclosure agreement, the purpose and data have been changed. Thus I will provide only a glance at my process.

@Margaret Rouse

About The Process:

To break up the process into digestible chunks - Iâ€™ve grouped the users by the impact of shared pain points, creating three behavioral lines: Green line (user type 1), Orange line (user type 2) and Blue line (user type 3). Each line is optimized for a specific problem (pain point). To achieve desired results this task required detailed creation and validation of personas. After working through all lines separately I needed to find the best approach to find a solution that suits all three. Next step of the process was combining/overlapping all three lines and discovering intersections. Those intersections were the key sections that solved the problem for all three user types and needed to be focused on the most.

Final Steps:

After detecting all key interactions it was time to test the hypothesis with lo-fi wireframes. Indeed when I mapped out behavioral lines with the wireframes it became obvious which screens are the most crucial for the task.

Final Takeaways:

While all stages in product design are equally important, a well refined research process can save time, energy and valuable resources later in the process. Not many companies can provide a significant budget for early stage research, that is why it is important to find the best suitable research strategy for the provided task.
