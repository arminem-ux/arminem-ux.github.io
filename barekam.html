About The Company:

Barekam Logistics is an international shipping organization operating since 2017.

Company mission is borderless shopping and bringing closer families by enabling easy cross border parcel shipment

Introduction:

As a UX designer, I was tasked with enhancing the online shopping and shipping experience that facilitates cross-border shopping for its users. The company provides users with a warehouse address where they can ship their purchased goods and then have them delivered directly to their desired location.

Primary User Dashboard

Objective:

The primary goal of this project was to streamline the user journey,
particularly focusing on simplifying the registration and order creation process,based on feedback gathered from active users.
Additionally, we aimed to address the needs of a significant portion of our user base, as 48% of them were identified as not being very tech-savvy.

Research and Data Gathering:

To understand the pain points and preferences of our users, I conducted user interviews, surveys, and analyzed customer support interactions.

Additionally, I utilized analytics data to identify drop-off points and areas of friction within the existing system. It was crucial to gather insights from both tech-savvy and non-tech-savvy users to ensure inclusivity in the redesign process.

Key Findings: * Complexity in registration and order creation process.
* Confusion regarding shipping options and costs.
* Lack of clarity in tracking shipments.
* Technical difficulties faced by non-tech-savvy users.

Online Shopper User Flows

Design Iterations:

Based on the gathered data, I proposed several design iterations aimed at addressing the identified pain points while keeping the user experience intuitive and accessible.

Simplified Registration Process:

* Reduced the number of required fields in the registration form.
* Implemented guided tooltips and inline help texts to assist users throughout the registration process.
* Introduced a step-by-step wizard interface to break down the process into manageable chunks
* Streamlined Order Creation:
* Introduced a unified dashboard where users can manage their shipments and orders.
* Implemented a one-click ordering feature for frequently purchased items.
* Improved visibility of shipping options and costs upfront to avoid surprises during checkout.

Design Iterations:

Enhanced Tracking System:

* Revamped the shipment tracking interface with clear status indicators and real-time updates.
* Integrated proactive notifications via email and SMS to keep users informed about their shipment's progress.

Accessibility Improvements:

* Implemented larger fonts, clearer labels, and simplified layouts to accommodate users with varying levels of technical proficiency.
* Conducted usability testing with non-tech-savvy participants to ensure the redesigned interface was easy to navigate.

Conclusion:

By prioritizing user feedback and focusing on inclusivity, we were able to optimize the cross-border shopping experience for both tech-savvy and non-tech-savvy users.
The iterative design process allowed us to continuously refine the platform, ensuring it met the evolving needs of our diverse user base.
Moving forward, we will continue to monitor user feedback and iterate on the design to maintain a seamless and intuitive experience.
